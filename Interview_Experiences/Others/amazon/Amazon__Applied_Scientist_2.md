## Overview
Recently went through the full interview loop for an Applied Scientist 2 role at Amazon. Sharing the structure and what actually mattered.

## Interview Process

### Screening Round
- **Format:** Online Assessment
- **Duration:** [Duration not specified]
- **Topics Covered:**
  - 1 DSA (LeetCode Medium – Sliding Window)
  - Discussion on past ML project
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Basic problem-solving ability
  - Whether you actually understand your project end-to-end

### Round 1
- **Format:** Technical Interview
- **Duration:** [Duration not specified]
- **Topics Covered:**
  - 2 DSA questions
    - Binary Search (on answer space)
    - Stack (monotonic stack pattern)
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Clear thinking
  - Clean implementation
  - Ability to optimize step by step

### Round 2
- **Format:** Technical Interview
- **Duration:** [Duration not specified]
- **Topics Covered:**
  - 1 DSA (Graph – BFS based problem)
  - Deep dive into past projects
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Why this model?
  - Why not something simpler?
  - Trade-offs
  - Evaluation approach

### Round 3 – ML System Design
- **Format:** System Design Interview
- **Duration:** [Duration not specified]
- **Topics Covered:**
  - This was the most important round
  - Structured approach expected:
    - Clear problem understanding (use case + constraints)
    - Data pipeline (collection, cleaning, feature engineering)
    - Model selection (baseline → advanced, with reasoning)
    - Training strategy (offline/online, retraining)
    - Evaluation (business-aligned metrics, A/B testing)
    - Deployment (batch vs real-time, latency considerations)
    - Scalability (handling large traffic, distributed setup)
    - Monitoring (data drift, performance degradation, feedback loop)
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Less about fancy models, more about building something that actually works in production

### Round 4 – ML + Behavioral
- **Format:** Technical + Behavioral Interview
- **Duration:** [Duration not specified]
- **Topics Covered:**
  - ML fundamentals
  - Past experience
  - Amazon Leadership Principles
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Ownership
  - Decision-making
  - Clarity of thought

## Key Learnings
- Amazon DSA rounds were mostly Medium level
- Not extremely intense like Google-level interviews
- If your fundamentals are clear, it's manageable

## Tips & Advice
- **DSA:** Be solid with Medium-level problems
- **ML:** Strong fundamentals + real project depth
- **System Design:** Think in terms of production systems
- **Communication:** This makes a huge difference
- **Balance:** Most people over-focus on just one area. The role actually demands a balance across all.

## Preparation Resources
- LeetCode Medium problems
- ML fundamentals review
- System design practice
- Amazon Leadership Principles
